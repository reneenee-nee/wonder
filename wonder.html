For parents

Wonder is for curious children and the grown-ups beside them.

Your child sees a snail crossing the path and wants to know why it leaves a trail. Or they ask why the sky goes dark, or whether volcanoes can erupt underwater. You type the question in. Wonder finds an honest answer — not a simplified one.

Wonder isn't a teacher, and it isn't trying to be. It's a knowledge companion: something to think alongside. The curiosity belongs to your child. Wonder just helps it go somewhere.

Wonder is what a child deserves from a piece of technology. Not a reward. Not a score. Not a profile. A real answer to a real question, given with respect.

There are no accounts here. No streaks, no dashboards, nothing to unlock. Wonder doesn't remember your child between visits and doesn't track anything. Each question is its own moment.

Every answer ends with one optional follow-up question — a door your child can choose to open, or walk past. You're in charge of where the conversation goes next.

How does Wonder know things?

Wonder is powered by AI — which means it has read an enormous number of books, science papers, and articles. It isn't magic. It's a kind of very well-read thinking machine. It can make mistakes, but it's very good at explaining how the natural world works.

About Wonder

AI as a thinking companion, not an answer machine.

Wonder is a simple web app for children aged 7–9, used together with a parent. A child asks a question out loud about the natural world — animals, weather, space, the human body. The parent types it in. Wonder responds honestly, without talking down to the child.

Every answer ends with one optional follow-up question. A door to open, or not. The child decides.

Most tools optimise for completion. Wonder optimises for curiosity. The goal is not to end the child's wondering — it is to deepen it.
